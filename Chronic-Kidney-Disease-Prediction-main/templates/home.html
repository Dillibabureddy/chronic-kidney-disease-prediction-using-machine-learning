Chronic Kidney Disease Prediction

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is an important worldwide health issue since it is associated with high morbidity and mortality. It is important to diagnose CKD at an early stage in order to stop it from getting to worse stages. In this paper, we investigated several machine learning models in order to make the prediction of CKD more precise and employed algorithms such as XGBoost, Support Vector Machine (SVM), Linear Regression, Random Forest, Decision Tree, Logistic Regression and Na¨ıve Bayes. Age, gender and laboratory test results among health data were processed with the aim of making sure the data physically and logically makes sense. Each model was then assessed against the major metrics, namely accuracy, precision, sensitivity and specificity. Findings showed that Random forest model was the most accurate among the other with a 96.67% of accuracy obtained. Furthermore, sensitivity was found to be highest for Naive Bayes 95.8% and XGBoost at 91.66% which was useful in detecting CKD cases. This study has demonstrated the efficacy of the machine learning models in CKD prediction in which Hansel forest showed high precision levels while Naive bayes and XGboost showed high performance in sensitivity. These results are encouraging when it comes to the early detection and management of CKD, which can result in good outcomes in patients.
